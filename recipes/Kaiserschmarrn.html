Kaiserschmarrn

Description

Kaiserschmarrn is a traditional Austrian dessert made from a fluffy, lightly sweet pancake that is shredded into bite-sized pieces while cooking. It’s typically dusted with powdered sugar and served with fruit compote, such as plum or apple sauce. Soft on the inside with a slightly caramelized exterior, Kaiserschmarrn is a comforting and classic treat often enjoyed as a dessert or even a sweet main dish.

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 250 ml milk
  • 150 g all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 packet vanilla sugar (optional)
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 30 g butter (for frying)
  • 30 g butter (for frying)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Separate the eggs into yolks and whites.
  2. Mix the egg yolks with milk, flour, sugar, vanilla sugar, and salt until smooth.
  3. Beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  4. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the batter.
  5. Melt butter in a large pan over medium heat.
  6. Pour the batter into the pan and cook until the bottom is golden brown.
  7. Flip the pancake, cook the other side, then tear it into bite-sized pieces.
  8. Lightly caramelize the pieces for a few more minutes.
  9. Sprinkle with powdered sugar and serve warm.
